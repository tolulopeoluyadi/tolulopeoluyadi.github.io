01 Project Management & Process Innovation

Case IQ Implementation

Technical Business Analyst / Program Manager — APL Legal case management for Ethics & Compliance, Equal Employment Opportunity, and Accommodations.

The Before State

Intake and tracking relied on forms, email follow-ups, and manual spreadsheets. Status visibility was limited, handoffs inconsistent, and reporting required time-consuming cleanup.

Why This Is Complex

  • Multiple services with different workflows and confidentiality needs
  • High bar for defensibility: audit trails, role-based access
  • Different user perspectives: requesters vs. admins
  • Change management during active operations
01 Solution Design

From Email + Spreadsheets → Structured System

Goal: Clear workflow steps, real-time visibility, and built-in measurement from day one.

What Changed

Both sides see where a case is in the process. Sub-statuses reduce ambiguity and improve handoffs. Templated emails, SSO login, tracked changes, and operational dashboards for volume, backlog, cycle time, and bottlenecks — all built-in.

Shared Status Taxonomy

Defined what statuses mean across services and what actions move a case forward.

Standardization vs. Flexibility

Enough structure for reporting and auditability without blocking real workflows.

Data Quality from Day One

Required fields, controlled vocabulary, and consistent timestamps designed upfront.

Feedback Loop

Pilot → measure → refine sub-statuses and templates. Iterative improvement baked in.

02 Measurement & Evaluation

Janney Grants Evaluation

Evaluation planning and data workstream lead — summative evaluation of Janney Grants (2022–2025), a staff-driven innovation program since 1984.

The Problem

Janney Grants have funded innovation for decades, but the program had never undergone formal outcome assessment. Leadership needed actionable insight on where value concentrates and how to strengthen the program.

Why Measurement Is Hard

  • Outputs are diverse: publications, conferences, international collaborations
  • Impact is multi-dimensional — not just counts
  • Attribution is tricky: outcomes appear months later

My Ownership

  • Compiled + cleaned historical dataset (2022–2025) for analysis and sampling
  • Created focus/interview criteria and grouping for representative coverage
  • Drafted survey/interview instruments and data collection workflows
  • Supported logic model framing (inputs → outputs → outcomes)
  • Introduced AI-assisted workflow for scaling qualitative coding with review controls
  • Built end-to-end “idea to award” process overview for applicants
